Mozyr Castle

Wooden defensive and administrative building of the late Middle Ages in Mozyr. It occupied a promontory between wide ravines that cut through the high root bank of Pripyat. The tetrahedral towers had seven combat tiers with sub-towers, and were covered with a hipped roof from above.The wall with the tower had two combat tiers and also had a sub-section with a combat gallery. Two townships were adapted for residential purposes.

After the reconstruction in 1576, it had 5 towers. In 1609 it completely burned down, in 1613 it was restored. During the military operations of the XVII century , the castle was repeatedly destroyed and gradually fell into disrepair.

In the XVIII century, the Mozyr Castle was a ceremonial castle and palace complex, which consisted of a wooden palace in the center and an economic zone.

On September 16, 2005, the decorative and historical center "Mozyr Castle" was opened, which is a decorated unique museum, where the entire landscape area with architectural and planning buildings is an exposition. Three towers housed expositions: "Armory", "Hunting", "Administrative" The castle was destroyed and restored. He suffered greatly in 1919 during the Strekopytovsky mutiny and during the Great Patriotic War.
